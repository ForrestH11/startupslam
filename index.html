What

A 1Day series of Workshops for UVic Students to see a glimpse into the life of the Tech startup world. Three sessions with two workshop choices in each session for students to choose from. Each workshop will have a presentation, interactive segment, and information & links for student to explore post workshop. There will be three rotations of time slots to provide students with as much context as possible.

The workshops will be run by different companies who have volunteered their time and put together the interactive workshops in order to expand on what students are learning at UVic.

Techniques for Prototyping Game AI
TinyMob

For this session we will walk through TinyMob’s process for prototyping out games and creating simple but effective AI systems We will introduce techniques for creating a flexible prototype that can be used to test out ideas. For the hands-on portion, students will be building the AI for one of the enemies in a game prototype. We will walk them through how to build a game’s AI in layers. By the end of the workshop students will be able to test their AI systems against other students to see which survives the longest.

Who

We expect 30-40 students per workshop form UVic’s Computer science, Computer Engineering, Software Engineering, and Business Schools. We are also inviting students from local colleges to attend. Students will range from newcomers to experienced developers.

Why

We think it is important for students to see what is actually being used by local tech companies and what employers expect from new graduates. This workshop aims at giving students a short introduction into skills that are highly sought after in the startup world as well as expose them to new ideas and technologies that can be pursued outside of the classroom. This is also an opportunity for companies to showcase their product and ideas to students.
