What

Sendwithus is hosting Startup Slam v2.0! On October 3rd 2015 you are invited to come and connect with local tech leaders who are leading the way in their respective fields. MetaLab, Pretio Interactive, TinyMob Games, Sendwithus OneNet Marketing & Flytographer are all hosting workshops ranging from advanced AI prototyping to Bootstrapping your own business. What will you learn at Startup Slam v2.0?

The workshops are free for students and developers to attend. Lunch will be served to quench your hunger and coffee to keep you energized. So what are you waiting for, register now to get your Startup Slam v2.0 T-shirt (limited supplies).

Speakers

Section 1
Customer Acquisition 101
One Net Marketing - Dylan Tousen CMO

How to acquire and retain customers with pirate marketing. In this fast-paced workshop we'll show how to acquire and retain customers using the proven AARRR pirate marketing model. We'll then put our skills to work and create live ad campaigns complete with conversion tracking. To maximize value of this workshop, participants should have access to a website and the ability to edit the sites HTML code. Grouping is encouraged.

Wake me up, before you golang?
Sendwithus - Gregory Schier Principal Engineer

What is “Go”? Is it more than a title to create bad puns or is it something that can create amazing code so fast that you can “Go” to production faster and easier than ever before. Come and see what Sendwithus does with golang and how it is changing our app, you may even learn how to write some code and build some stuff.

Section 2
Zero to Slack in 30 Minutes
Metalab - Jason Trill Senior Developer, Izaak Schroeder Senior Developer, & James Philips Senior Developer

Participants will learn how to use cutting edge technologies that offer a top-notch developer experience to build a real-time frontend web application. Led by three senior engineers from MetaLab, we will be exploring technologies like Babel (ES6/7), React, Redux (Devtools), and Webpack+HMR.

Techniques for Prototyping Game AI
TinyMob - Chris Hoefgen Founder/CTO & Graham Davis Lead Game Developer

For this session we will walk through TinyMob’s process for prototyping out games and creating simple but effective AI systems We will introduce techniques for creating a flexible prototype that can be used to test out ideas. For the hands-on portion, students will be building the AI for one of the enemies in a game prototype. We will walk them through how to build a game’s AI in layers. By the end of the workshop students will be able to test their AI systems against other students to see which survives the longest.

Section 3
Customer Experience as a Secret Weapon for Growth
Flytographer - Nicole Smith Founder/CEO
  • Designing the ideal experience (customer journey storyboarding)
  • Optimizing the experience (responding/learning/iterating/improving)
  • Growth hacking (customer experience as a word-of-mouth marketing tool)
    • Git: Workflows, Teams, and Integrations
    Pretio Interactive - Rob O’Dwyer CTO & Aurora Walker Software Developer

    The popular revision control system git could be your new friend, or if you're already been introduced, your bestest of best buds. Let Aurora perform a full introduction, so that instead of just being nodding acquaintances in the hallway, git becomes the kind of friend who would bail you out of jail.
